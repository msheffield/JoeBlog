Every Dog Has Its Day

June 23, 2017

Cancer update #16

This was a great off week for me. My symptoms basically went away on Monday. I have only had the chemo taste when I don't eat enough or stay hydrated, so that is what I have been trying to do. Food, water, and excercise have been the best things for me.

Speaking of exercise, our new dog has been a big help in that regard. I have been going on tons of walks with him, and taking him to the dog park and running around with him. He has really helped me stay active, and for that, I am really grateful. He is a sweetheart, and even though he has a few peccadillos, he is a really good companion to us. We went up to Bellingham for father's day and he met my folks dog Calvin. They wore each other out playing. For anyone going through cancer or illness and needing motivation to get outside, get some fresh air, and get some exericise, getting a dog has been amazing for me.

Other than that, this week has been great for hanging out with friends and family. Friday we went to our friend Eddie's surprise 30th birthday which was awesome. Mike came down on Tuesday and he, Cristina, Andy, Kimberly, and my buddy Evan went and did trivia at Ravenna Brewing, and we won! I fell in love with pub trivia in Houston at my friends Donna and Deb's bar, and I have continued doing that here in Seattle. Yesterday me and Coop and Brett got out and enjoyed the nice weather we are having here in Seattle with a nice long walk around our neighborhood. So great to spend time with the folks I love while I am feeling healthy.

The only other cancer news is we are waiting on some test results to see if my Obamacare insurance will cover some promising new immunotherapy called Keytruda. They are looking at my tumor to see if it has a mismatch repair deficiency. If I do then fingers crossed I can get on this drug as well. Chances aren't great, I think only about 5% of cancers have this, but its something giving me some hope these past few weeks. Speaking of Obamacare, for my friends in states with Republican Senators, if your politics allow you to, feel free to give them a call and urge them to vote against this terrible repeal bill. Its the one thing making me very nervous/scared/angry/sad right now. Obamacare has been a literal lifesaver for me.

Thats it for this blog I think. Been getting in touch with my angsty teen self and listening to a bunch of emo revival. This has been tops on my list this week, the lyrics are depressing but I love The Hotelier so y'all can deal with it! Life is good right now, the weather is nice, the Mariners are above .500, and I'm feeling great.

Thanks for reading!

Love, Joe
