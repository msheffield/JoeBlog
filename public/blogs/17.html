The Mountains are Calling and I Must Go.

May 26, 2017

Cancer update # 13

I am currently writing this blog from my grandma and grandpas houst in Santa Rosa, CA. We have a ton of family here so apoligies but this blog might be a short one. This was an off chemo week for me and it was a pretty fantastic one. I have had very few symptoms this week. The only ones that seem to persist for any length of time are the gross taste and the neuropathy.

This week my family rented a church van and headed down for California. We avoided I5 as much as possible and took an alternate route down through Eastern Oregon, into Reno, and eventually on to Yosemite. I love visiting our national parks, and this was my first time in Yosemite. The drive in is breathtaking. Here is a photo of Cristina and I as we approached the park, with Half Dome blurry in the background.

Entering Yosemite! #yosemitenationalpark

We stayed in an Airbnb just outside the park called the Howling Wolf Lodge. It was kitchy as hell and super fun. We spend the three and a half days hiking throughout the park. We did two major hikes, the first was up the Mist Trail. This was a spectacular hike that takes you up through Vernal and Nevada Fall. The water level in Yosemite is at near historic levels currently and so the falls were raging, creating a ton of mist to walk through. We took a nice photo at the top. Please ignore my sweaty belly button.

Top of Vernal Fall. This country is goddamn beautiful. #misttrail #vernalfall #yosemite #fuckcancer

The next day we ventured up to Glacier Point to take in the amazing views of the park. It really is stunning. Sorry that my scruffy face is getting in the way of the beautiful girl in all these photos and the beautful scenery.

Unreal. #yosemite #elcapitan #halfdome #america

A post shared by Joe M (@beverlyfreaks) on May 23, 2017 at 11:00am PDT

The last day, we hiked to Upper Yosemite Falls. This is a real burner of a hike: near continuous switchbacks help you gain about 2700 feet in elevation in a very short period. It was super strenuous but it felt so good get through it and make it to the top a week after getting a chemo treatment. I was so happy to be up there with my family surrounded by the incredible beauty this country has to offer. I was also super proud of my mom, who is a breast cancer survivor. The hike was super tough, so I took this video at the top to celebrate.

This week was a reminder to me about how important it is to work to protect this amazing planet we live on. Our national parks are a treasure. I love this country, despite its faults and current administration, and while I love celebrating on the 4th of July, but nothing makes me prouder to be an American than going to our parks and seeing all the important conservation work that is going on around the country.

Anyways, how awesome is this Girlpool song?

Thats all I have for this week. I'm going to go enjoy some quality time with the fam.

Love, Joe
