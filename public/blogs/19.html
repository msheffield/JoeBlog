Cancer update #15

Sorry I missed last week! Cristina and I decided to go camping, which was very much needed, so I missed last week. The last two weeks have brought a bunch of good change to our lives. Had a nice off chemo week last week and we bought a new car! We went with a Subaru Forester. It is time to sell the truck, which is way too big for Seattle. I am still having neuropathy and still having weird tastes in my mouth. I looked in the mirror the other day and holy hell my hair is thinning. Thankfully I was blessed with a large head and an incredibly thick head of hair so I have a lot to lose. It is very weird every time I am in the shower and I run my hands through my hair and they are just covered in hair that has fallen out.

Also last week we went to our 5th Mariners game. The tickets were gifted to us by my very generous former teacher Laurie Mullarky. Thank you Laurie and Craig! Cristina and I are somehow 5-0 at Mariners games this year. Last week we drove all over the greater Seattle area looking for a dog to adopt! We went to 5 animal shelters. They were all packed with dogs, and there were lots of adorable ones, but in the end we didn't find the perfect buddy to adopt. Lots of suffering dogs that probably need more medical or emotional care than we can provide. Hopefully they can find nice homes with big yards and families with the means to help. We were undeterred though, and last Friday I went out again and found the perfect dog. He still needed to be neutered, so we put in our paperwork and waited until Tuesday, when we could take him home. He is a gentle, sweet, intelligent, affectionate 5 month old guy named Cooper. We love him very much.

Adopted this guy from @paws_wa today! He is sweet, friendly, smart, and loves belly rubs. Couldn't be happier with the newest Wolf-McClenaclan. Welcome home Cooper!

I had chemo this week and he provided some much needed snuggles after I was disconnected on Thursday. This week's chemo didn't give me really any of the bad symptoms except I felt extremely fatigued. I think my body is getting tired of it, but the exercise I can get on the off weeks is doing good things helping me cope. Here is Coop and I asleep on our couch on Thursday:

Andy was my chemo buddy this week. Thanks Andy! To pass the time during chemo I helped him study for the bar, since I'm pretty much an expert, and we had a nice nurse that provided some good laughs. Here is a little snap story of the day and some shots of the new pup we picked up afterwards!

So lots of big changes this week! Cristina and I have always talked about wanting a dog, and getting a new car and selling the truck, and doing a bunch more camping and traveling. I think we pushed ourselves to get some of that done this week because, as I talked about before, now its all about living out our bucket list and saying yes more. We probably could have waited a bit to do some of this stuff but life is too short.

Thinking about cancer this week, Cristina showed me a quote from Thoreau that I thought was nice:

  • There is one consolation in being sick; and that is the possibility that you may recover to a better state than you were ever in before.

I think this is pretty true. I plan on getting healthy and beating this tumor, and it is a great consolation to me that I know I will be in a better state than before. I feel like I've even started the process of getting to a better state already. Why wait til I'm healthy? Being sick sucks, the treatment sucks, but everything else about my life is so much better right now. I feel wrapped in love by my friends and family, I feel really healthy and strong during my off chemo weeks, and I'm living my life more deliberately and purposfully.

Been listening to a lot of new tunes lately. Here is a great one for your enjoyment.

Anyways, remember that it is Father's Day Sunday! My dad is pretty great. Love you dad!

Thanks for reading!

Love, Joe
