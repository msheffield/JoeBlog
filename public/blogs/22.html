On the cancer front, this last week was not the best. Normally this is a chemo week for me. I had been having some greater difficulty swallowing in the last couple weeks, and some of my bloodwork had some alarming numbers, so my doctor pushed up my scan to this Monday. Unfortunately, it looks like the chemo has stopped being effective, and the tumor is growing again. My doctor took me off chemo, and I am currently trying to get into a clinical trial that is going on right now.

The clinical trial is an immunotherapy trial for a drug called Nivolumab in combination with another barely pronouncable drug called Ipilimumab. Try saying that one a few times. I will either be getting those two drugs, or the Nivolumab and a drug lovingly named "BMS-98016". I discussed this in a blog awhile back but Nivolumab is a checkpoint inhibitor that against the PD-1 protein in my tumor. This is about where my understanding starts to fail, but hopefully my tumor responds to this treatment! Side effects apparently could be: sever skin irritation, inflamed lungs, diabetes, and thyroid problems. So apologies in advance if I start flaking skin and coughing everywhere. As I experienced my last time trying to get into a clinical trial, I will not consider myself in the trial until I am actually getting the drugs pumped into my chest. Hopefully my tumor doesn't start bleeding before then.

So thats the bad news. I have a growing problem. How I'm feeling this week:

  • Disappointed. I'm disappointed that the chemo stopped working. I was pretty overwhelmed meeting my doc on Tuesday to go over the results. All these weeks of feeling like shit, only to have the tumor start growing again. Thankfully it shrunk pretty significantly initially, but I would have loved to keep that going. My doctor was also disappointed: she says usually folks that have such great results after the first scan continue to have good results.
  • Scared. There aren't a lot of treatments out there for this cancer, and to check one off the list and still have this tumor is pretty scary. Cristina and I were watching some of the travel videos I've made of our trips together and I'm scared I won't be able to go on more big adventures with her. I'm scared about missing out on a lot of things.
  • Hopeful. I'm hopeful for that this clinical trial produces results. I'm hopeful that I can beat the odds and start shrinking this tumor again. I'm going to do everything I can to keep my immune system running in overdrive: exercising, getting outside, eating well, and enjoying life.

To wrap up this blog, I had a nice chat over some beers with a friend, Nattalie, about how helpful positive thinking is. Yes, I was disappointed, yes, I am kind of scared, but I still think the best thing I can do for myself is to not fall into thinking about "why me". Who knows "why me"? I'm going to continue to enjoy everything I can: every snuggle with Coop, every date with Cristina, every meaningful conversation over a pint with great friends, every visit from family, every sunrise and sunset. I can't let any negativity get in the way of that.
